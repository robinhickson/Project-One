What is air traffic management?

Air traffic management (ATM) is a broad term used to describe integration of all processes and associated technologies serving aircraft from departure, through terminal area and en-route airspace volumes, to the airport of destination.

What are the main objectives of ATM?

To ensure that aircraft can operate safely, efficiently and expeditiously.

What services are included within ATM?

The complete range of services is vast, but can be summarised as three major activities:

  • Air Traffic Services (ATS)
  • Airspace Management (ASM)
  • Air Traffic Flow and Capacity Management (ATFCM)

Interested in ATM?

You can find out much more about ATM in Europe by visiting Eurocontrol's website. Eurocontrol (The European Organisation for the Safety of Air Navigation) is an international organisation working to achieve safe and seamless air traffic management across Europe.
